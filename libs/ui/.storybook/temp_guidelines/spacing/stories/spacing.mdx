It involves sizing and lining up elements in multiples of 8 or factors of 8 (4, 2).

## Best Practices

Every detail in a UI, including the empty space, counts.
The distance between elements forms meaningful relationships and establishes a clear hierarchy.<br/>

### Forming Relationships Through Spacing

Elements positioned closer to each other create a stronger relation. The more the space between components, the weaker the relation seems.<br/>
Consistent spacing across elements denotes them as interconnected pieces of equal importance.<br/>
Furthermore, spacing is a powerful tool to group associated information together. It intuitively segments content on a page, eliminating the need for lines or graphics as separators.

### Establishing Hierarchy Using Spacing

Greater spacing around an element implies its superior hierarchy. The more space encloses an element, the higher its prominence.<br/>
With headers, for example, the top-level ones have more space, enhancing their prominence and focus. <br/>
Headers of lesser importance have narrower spacing, signaling their secondary status.<br/>
Elements that are set close to each other can be easily overlooked. Users may see the grouping but not process each individual item.<br/>
Therefore, if you have an element or content of high importance on the page, consider giving it extra surrounding space to help it attract focus.

### The Role of White Space

White space, is as crucial as any other element. It serves as a visual rest area in the heavy traffic of information processing.<br/>
An overload of dense information can cause disorientation or overwhelm. So, while sections of a UI can be dense, they should never overload the entire page.<br/>
There should be adequate white space for the user's eyes to relax.
